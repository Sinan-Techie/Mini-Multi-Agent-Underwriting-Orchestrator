# Health Screening Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

This document describes the screening procedure used by the underwriting team
to assess applicants for individual term-life insurance policies. It is the
source of truth for the questions a screening agent must ask. The agent should
retrieve the relevant section based on the applicant's prior answers and ask
the next appropriate question.

---

## 1. General health

Always begin with the general-health section before moving to specific risk
factors. The intent is to anchor the conversation in the applicant's overall
self-assessment before drilling into specifics.

- Ask the applicant to describe their general health on a scale of 1 (poor)
  to 5 (excellent).
- If the applicant answers 1 or 2, follow up with: *"What is the primary
  factor affecting your health?"*
- If the applicant answers 5, no follow-up is needed in this section.

---

## 2. Lifestyle — tobacco

Tobacco use is the single largest lifestyle risk factor and must always be
asked.

- Ask: *"Do you currently use any form of tobacco — cigarettes, vapes, cigars,
  or chewing tobacco?"*
- If the answer is yes, follow up with: *"How many years have you used
  tobacco, and roughly how often per week?"*
- If the answer is no, follow up with: *"Have you used tobacco regularly in
  the past 12 months?"*

A "yes" to either question places the applicant in the elevated-risk tier
for pricing.

---

## 3. Lifestyle — alcohol

- Ask: *"How many alcoholic drinks do you consume in a typical week?"*
- A response of more than 14 drinks per week is flagged for review.
- If the applicant declines to answer, record this and move on. Do not press.

---

## 4. Pre-existing conditions

This section is conditional. Only ask these questions if the applicant rated
their general health below 4 in section 1, or indicated tobacco use in
section 2.

- Ask: *"Have you been diagnosed with any of the following conditions in the
  last 5 years: diabetes, heart disease, cancer, or chronic respiratory
  illness?"*
- If yes, ask the applicant to specify the condition and approximate year of
  diagnosis.
- If the condition is in remission or controlled by medication, note this. The
  underwriting team will weight it differently from an active diagnosis.

---

## 5. Family history

- Ask: *"Has any immediate family member (parent or sibling) been diagnosed
  with a serious heart condition or cancer before the age of 60?"*
- This question is asked of every applicant regardless of prior answers.

---

## 6. Activity and fitness

- Ask: *"How many hours per week do you engage in moderate to vigorous physical
  activity?"*
- Less than one hour per week is flagged as a sedentary lifestyle indicator.
- More than ten hours per week of high-intensity activity may also be flagged
  for review (extreme-sports risk).

---

## 7. Region-specific addendum — UAE / KSA

Applicants resident in the UAE or KSA must additionally be asked:

- *"Do you travel internationally for work more than 60 days per year?"*

Applicants resident in IND skip this question.

---

## 8. Closing the screening

Once the agent has covered sections 1, 2, 3, 5, 6 and the conditional
section 4 / 7, the screening is complete. The agent must summarise the
collected risk factors back to the applicant in plain language before
handing off to the quote agent. Do not give a price in the screening
agent — that is the quote agent's responsibility.
